the plain, everyday model is on Dashboard builder — this page is the options menu
Palette — option ⑥
Drag one onto the board you last touched (or click to add). Drag any tile OUT and release over this panel to remove it — one undo puts it back with its neighbours.
What each board shows
  • 1Two boards, one canvasDrag a tile from ① into ② — the source board settles, the target makes room live, and the whole crossing is ONE undo.
  • 2Explicit cell placementThe green tile declares fixed cells (gridItem col 3–4 · row 1–2); the others carry no cells and auto-flow around it.
  • 3Bounded sectionmaxRows: 1 nested inside board ③: its own pack grid, a locked slab outside. Pull a tile past it and the strip GROWS a row (all tiles together); pull back and it shrinks. The main dashboard deliberately does not use this.
  • 4Pinned tileThe amber tile is locked: never pushed, and a drag onto it is refused outright.
  • 5Float & fit/growFloat off = gravity packs upward, no holes; on = tiles stay where you drop them. Fit = board keeps its height, rows squeeze; grow = rows keep their height, the board extends.
  • 6Palette in, drag outDrag-in places live from the moment the cursor enters; a release over the palette deletes, undoably.
